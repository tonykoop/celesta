# Decision Record

## Decisions

- Readiness is `L1 concept packet`.
- The instrument is treated as a struck steel-bar idiophone with a keyboard
  action, not as a string piano variant.
- The first risks are hammer rebound, bar support, resonator matching, damper
  control, and action regulation.
- The packet intentionally avoids pitch ranges, bar dimensions, resonator
  dimensions, hammer weights, action ratios, and CAD/DXF outputs.
- Every visual-output register row is `concept_only`; no artifact is
  fabrication authority.

## Open Questions

- What bar material and heat treatment should a first coupon use?
- How should bars be supported so they speak freely but stay stable under felt
  hammer strikes?
- What hammer felt and contact shape gives a clear attack without harshness?
- Should resonator tests begin with individual boxes or a shared resonator
  rail?
- How should dampers lift and return without adding release noise?
- What keyboard action can be regulated simply in a prototype shop setting?
- Which measurements should be captured before deciding on any full pitch set?
- What service access is required for tuning, hammer voicing, and damper
  regulation?

## Rejected For L1

- No dimensions.
- No tuning table.
- No bar lengths or resonator sizes.
- No hammer weights or action ratios.
- No CAD, DXF, G-code, or fabrication drawing.
- No claim that the concept is ready to build.
