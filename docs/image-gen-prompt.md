# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, bar lengths, resonator sizes, hammer weights, action ratios, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished celesta prototype, shot at roughly 50mm: a compact upright keyboard cabinet in warm maple or birch with a satin finish, the hinged top lifted to reveal the sound source inside — a row of graduated polished steel bars suspended on cloth-cushioned supports above individual wooden resonator boxes, a bank of felt hammers and slim dampers ranked behind a piano-style black-and-white keyboard, brass and steel action hardware catching the light. Bench-scale instrument the size of a small upright keyboard, presented as a clean studio product shot on a neutral seamless background with soft directional studio light, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic workshop scene of the celesta being softly played during prototype testing: natural window light falls across an open service panel so the felt hammers, lifted dampers, suspended steel bars, and wooden resonator boxes are all visible inside the cabinet. A maker's hands rest on the keyboard with anatomically plausible posture, and small voicing tools and spare felt samples sit on the bench nearby. Quiet, focused regulation atmosphere, realistic wood and metal textures, shallow depth of field, environmental storytelling, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the celesta's signature mechanism: a single felt hammer rebounding cleanly off a polished steel bar that is suspended on a soft support point above a rectangular wooden resonator box, with one damper lifted just clear of the bar. Small action pivots and regulation screws are visible, raking light grazes across felt nap, brushed steel, and wood grain to show texture, high detail, no measurement marks.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, fake brand or maker marks, no strings or piano soundboard, no dimension callouts or measurement lines, no impossible hammer or action geometry; anatomically plausible hands with no extra fingers and no hands inside the action while a note is sounding.
